Project 03 / Research

AQI Monitoring UAV

A solar-powered drone designed to monitor air quality at scale — built during my undergraduate years at DTU, published in an IEEE journal, and supported by a Delhi State Government research grant.

Back to projects Home

The problem

Urban air quality monitoring relies on a sparse network of fixed ground stations — too few to capture the spatial and temporal variability of pollution, and too expensive to scale. Pollutant concentrations can differ significantly across short distances and short time windows, but the data infrastructure to detect and act on that granularity rarely exists. Growing concerns around air quality in large cities made this a meaningful problem to work on.

What I built

Designed and developed a solar-powered UAV capable of carrying multiple air quality sensors across variable terrain. UAVs offer a distinct advantage here: high manoeuvrability across both horizontal and vertical dimensions allows for high-spatial-resolution sampling and near-surface vertical profiling of atmospheric pollution — something fixed stations cannot do. The solar power system addressed the endurance limitation of conventional battery-only drones, enabling sustained operation without constant retrieval and recharging. The system integrated hardware design, embedded electronics, and multi-sensor data collection.

Outcome

The work was published in an IEEE conference paper — a rigorous peer-reviewed venue for engineering research. The project was also supported by a Delhi State Government research grant. The published research is available via IEEE Xplore (DOI 10.1109/ICCE.2019.8662050).

Why it matters

This project was my first experience taking a hardware system from a real-world problem through design, integration, and published research. Low-cost micro-sensors combined with UAV manoeuvrability make fine-grained, real-time air quality monitoring genuinely feasible — and the broader implications for smart city infrastructure are significant. It also taught me how much rigour the full arc from concept to publication actually requires.
