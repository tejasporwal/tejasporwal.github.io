Project 03 / Research

AQI Monitoring UAV

A solar-powered drone designed to monitor air quality at scale — built during my undergraduate years at DTU, published in an IEEE journal, and supported by a Delhi State Government research grant.

Back to projects Home

The problem

Air quality monitoring in Indian cities relies on a sparse network of fixed ground stations — too few to capture hyperlocal variation, and too expensive to scale quickly. Pollution levels can differ dramatically across short distances, but the data to act on that rarely exists.

What I built

Designed and built a solar-powered UAV capable of carrying AQI sensing equipment across variable terrain. The solar design addressed the endurance limitation of battery-only drones, making persistent monitoring feasible without constant retrieval and recharging. The system combined hardware design, embedded systems, and sensor integration.

Outcome

The research was published in an IEEE journal — one of the more rigorous publication venues for engineering work. The project was also recognised with a Delhi State Government grant in support of further development.

Why it matters

This project sits at the intersection of hardware, embedded systems, and a real environmental problem. It was my first experience taking a system from concept through to published research — and learning how much rigour that requires at every stage.
