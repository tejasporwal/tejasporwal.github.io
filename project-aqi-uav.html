Project 03 / Research

AQI Monitoring UAV

A fixed-wing solar-powered UAV and low-cost sensor stack designed to generate fine-grained, geo-tagged air-quality data for smart cities and pollution hotspot detection.

Back to work Home

The problem

Air quality is intensely local: pollution levels can change meaningfully from one point to another. Static monitoring stations are expensive, sparse, and often insufficient for capturing the spatial and temporal variation needed to identify pollution hotspots. In India, the paper notes that only a small fraction of census towns were monitored and that fixed stations were too costly to scale widely.

System design

Designed and manufactured a 3.12 metre fixed-wing UAV using carbon fibre rods, balsa wood, aramid, and glass fibre. The fixed-wing design was chosen over a quadcopter because it offered longer flight time and reduced propeller-wash interference with sensor readings. The prototype was designed around solar endurance, with a 38-cell SunPower C-60 module concept capable of generating around 100W under ideal conditions.

Data fusion module

Built a low-cost sensing module around an Alphasense OPC-R1 particulate sensor, Raspberry Pi, and Pixhawk flight controller. The system captured PM1, PM2.5, PM10, temperature, time, latitude, longitude, and altitude data. A custom Python script ran the OPC-R1 on Raspberry Pi, while GPS and flight data were exchanged with Pixhawk over Mavlink. The sensor sampling rate was set to one second, enabling spatiotemporal pollution mapping from mobile measurements.

Results

The UAV was successfully field-tested at low altitude with sufficient payload capacity for the data fusion module. The system generated PM2.5 readings linked to GPS coordinates, which were plotted using Python, Matplotlib, and Pandas to identify location-specific pollution variation. The data fusion module was also mounted on a car to demonstrate its use as a low-cost mobile monitoring station for public transportation systems and city-scale deployment.

Publication and relevance

The work was published as "Emerging Low-Cost Air Quality Monitoring Techniques for Smart Cities with UAV" at IEEE CONECCT 2020. The paper positions UAV-based sensing as a practical complement to scarce static stations and satellite methods, with applications in smart cities, industrial areas, glaciers, inaccessible terrain, and real-time stubble-burning monitoring. The published paper is available on IEEE Xplore, article 9198487.
