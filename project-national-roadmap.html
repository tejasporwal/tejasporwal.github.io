Project 01 / Strategy

National Technology Investment Roadmap

For a GCC nation’s IT ministry, I helped assess a very large opportunity set, narrow it into a sharper national thesis, and support a long-horizon investment agenda with real capital behind it.

Back to work Home

The problem

A national technology strategy can easily become too broad to act on. The real challenge was to move from a wide landscape of possibilities to a short list of technology areas that deserved national-level attention, funding, and execution discipline.

What I did

Screened roughly 150 technologies against a country-specific framework, combining technical reality, strategic relevance, implementation feasibility, and long-term economic upside. The goal was not just to identify attractive technologies, but to identify the ones that made sense for this specific national context.

Outcome

The work helped prioritize ten technologies for national focus and informed a long-horizon investment roadmap. More importantly, it created a usable decision structure rather than a one-off slide deck.

Why it matters

This kind of work sits at the edge of technology, policy, and execution. It requires enough technical depth to avoid fashionable nonsense and enough strategic discipline to convert insight into a portfolio of actual national bets.
