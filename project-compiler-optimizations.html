Project 02 / Engineering

Neural Network Compiler Optimizations

At Samsung Semiconductor India Research, I worked on patent-pending compiler and cache optimizations intended to make deep-learning inference faster and more efficient.

Back to work Home

The problem

Inference performance is often limited less by model architecture alone and more by how effectively the underlying compiler, memory access patterns, and execution pipeline are structured. Small gains here compound quickly at scale.

What I did

Worked on compiler and cache-level optimizations for neural network inference, focusing on how execution could be made more efficient under practical hardware constraints. The work demanded attention to system behavior, not just model behavior.

Outcome

The optimizations improved benchmark performance and efficiency by 15%+ and fed into patent-pending work. It was the kind of engineering that rewards careful thinking more than flashy demos.

Why it matters

This project is a good example of the part of engineering I enjoy most: moving below the interface layer and working on the systems assumptions that determine whether a model is merely interesting or actually deployable.
