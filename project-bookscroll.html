Project 02 / Co-Founder

Bookscroll

A quality-driven student utility marketplace built to make peer-to-peer buying and selling inside college more trusted, transparent, and economical.

Back to work Home

The problem

College students regularly need books, lab instruments, and hostel utilities such as coolers, but the resale market was fragmented and opaque. Informal shopkeepers operated on information asymmetry, often charging transaction margins of up to 100% between what one student sold an item for and what another student paid to buy it.

What I built

Co-founded Bookscroll as a trusted marketplace for students of the same college to find the goods they needed and connect directly with peers or seniors for the transaction. The idea was simple: basic student utilities, especially books, should be economical, and the transaction between students should be transparent and fair.

Trust and safety

The platform included hands-on trust mechanisms that made the marketplace practical for real users:

  • Manual grading of listed goods into "Good", "Better", and "Like new" based on seller-submitted images.
  • Manual intervention and resolution when there was a discrepancy in the condition of an exchanged item.
  • QR-code based UPI payments for transactions, adopted very early after UPI launched in 2016.
  • Escrow-style payment handling: money was held until the buyer confirmed the item's condition and marked the exchange as successful, after which the seller was paid.

What I learned

Bookscroll was a formative experience in directly interacting with buyers, building a technology-native platform, and serving a real customer need. It taught me that marketplace trust is not only a product feature; it is an operating system of policy, payment flow, dispute resolution, and user communication.
