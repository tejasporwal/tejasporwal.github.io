Project 02 / Founder

Bookscroll

A reading platform co-founded during college to make non-fiction more accessible — distilling books into digestible, scroll-friendly formats for time-constrained readers.

Back to projects Home

The problem

Most people who want to read more don't have the time to finish books. The knowledge is there, but the format — linear, dense, long — works against busy schedules. At the same time, existing summaries felt reductive: they stripped out the thinking, not just the length.

What I built

Co-founded Bookscroll to rethink how non-fiction is consumed. The product distilled books into structured, scrollable formats — preserving the key ideas and author voice without demanding hours of reading time. Building it meant thinking simultaneously about content curation, editorial structure, and distribution.

What I learned

This was an early lesson in the difference between a product that sounds good and one that users return to. Content quality and reading habit formation turned out to be harder problems than the platform itself — and more interesting for it.

Why it matters

Building Bookscroll alongside Recursion Academy gave me dual operating experience very early: one product about employment outcomes, the other about knowledge access. Both shaped how I think about user incentives and the gap between intent and behaviour.
