Project 03 / Founder

Recursion Academy

Recursion Academy was an income-share tech bootcamp I co-founded while still early in my career. It was one of the first places I learned what building for real users and real outcomes actually feels like.

Back to work Home

The problem

There was a gap between motivated learners and the kinds of outcomes-focused technical training that could actually move their careers forward. The usual alternatives were expensive, generic, or poorly aligned to placement outcomes.

What I built

Co-founded an income-share tech bootcamp designed around practical learning and employability rather than just content delivery. That meant thinking about curriculum, distribution, learner economics, and placement quality all at once.

Outcome

Recursion Academy reached 50+ paid learners, produced placements at companies such as Microsoft and Adobe, and was recognized as a DTU-IIF Top 5 startup with a reported Rs 1.2 Cr valuation.

Why it matters

This was an early operating education in itself. It taught me about incentives, user trust, unit economics, and the difference between an idea that sounds good and one that survives contact with customers.
